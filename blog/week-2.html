NYC nightlife, Broadway deals & food festivals – Week of Jan 8, 2024

Broadway & Bites: NYC’s Cultural Feast (Jan 8–14, 2024)

Published: January 12, 2024

This second week of January was all about indulgence—for your mind, body, and stomach. New York City lit up with Broadway deals, fine dining steals, winter lights, and indoor escapes. Here’s what had the city buzzing:

1. Broadway Week: Theater for All

Broadway Week kicked off Jan 9 with two-for-one ticket deals across major productions like *Hamilton*, *Moulin Rouge!*, and *Six*. New Yorkers and tourists alike flocked to Midtown, making theater more accessible than ever. For prime picks, many ticket buyers used TodayTix or official NYC & Company links.

2. NYC Restaurant Week: Prix Fixe Magic

Running simultaneously, Restaurant Week launched Jan 9 with $30–$60 prix-fixe menus at hundreds of top-rated eateries. From Union Square Café to L’Artusi in the West Village, diners enjoyed gourmet meals without breaking the bank. Tip: lunch menus often offered the best value.

3. Jazz Fest at Winter Jazzfest

The Winter Jazzfest returned Jan 10–18, anchoring its performances in Greenwich Village and SoHo. Highlights included Esperanza Spalding’s surprise set at Le Poisson Rouge and a late-night jam at Zinc Bar. For music lovers, this was the cultural heartbeat of the week.

4. Museum Hopping to Beat the Chill

As temps dipped into the 20s, crowds moved indoors. The Metropolitan Museum of Art debuted its “Lineages” exhibition, exploring fashion through centuries. Over at the Whitney, “Inheritance” captivated visitors with generational Black art and history. Free Friday nights continued at MoMA and Neue Galerie.

5. NYC Lights Festival in Brooklyn

Brooklyn Botanic Garden’s Lightscape enchanted families and couples alike through Jan 14. This immersive walk-through light show with synchronized music and warm cider made Prospect Heights a glow-in-the-dark date night hotspot.

6. Cozy Winter Bars

Venues like Refinery Rooftop and Serra Alpina (Eataly Flatiron’s alpine rooftop) leaned into the winter aesthetic with heated igloos, fur throws, and seasonal cocktails. Mulled wine, boozy hot chocolate, and après-ski vibes gave nightlife a toasty twist.

7. Ice Skating Highlights

Though the holidays were over, rinks like Bryant Park, Wollman Rink, and Rockefeller Center remained open. The lines thinned, making it the perfect week for spontaneous skating with skyline views.

NYNightlife Tips for the Week

  • Book Broadway early: The best seats vanish fast during Broadway Week. Weeknight shows offer better availability.
  • Lunch wins Restaurant Week: Prices are lower, portions generous, and easier to reserve.
  • Layer up: You’ll still want warm layers, even for rooftop bars with heaters.
