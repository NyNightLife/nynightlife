Kick Off 2024: Best Things to Do in NYC (Jan 1–7, 2024)

Published: January 5, 2024

Starting the year right—in true NYNightlife style—with rooftop revelry, cultural gems, and cool winter vibes. Here’s how locals and visitors rang in the first week of 2024:

1. Post-New Year’s Eve Skyline Party at 230 Fifth

The rooftop at 230 Fifth kept the energy alive on Jan 1 with live DJs, cocktails, and breathtaking skyline views—perfect for those who partied too hard on New Year’s Eve.

2. Winter Village at Bryant Park

Through mid-January, the Winter Village returned to Bryant Park with an ice rink, holiday shops, cozy igloos and curling lanes—a classic daytime-to-evening hangout.

3. Gospel Sunday at Harlem’s Abyssinian Baptist

Sunday services at the Abyssinian Baptist Church offered free, soul‑stirring gospel music—ideal for a spiritual yet lively start to 2024.

4. Coney Island Polar Bear Plunge

On Jan 1, thrill‑seekers jumped into freezing Atlantic waters at the iconic Polar Bear Plunge on Coney Island. A bold way to embrace New Year’s adventure.

5. 47th Annual Three Kings Day Parade

Held on Jan 5, the festive Three Kings Day Parade in East Harlem featured vibrant floats, dancing, and community spirit to honor a Latinx tradition.

6. NYC Hotel Week & Under the Radar Festival

NYC Hotel Week kicked off Jan 3, offering discounted stays, while the acclaimed Under the Radar Festival began Jan 5 at Public Theater, showcasing avant‑garde theater through Jan 21.

7. Broadway Week & Restaurant Week Arrival

Starting around Jan 7, Broadway Week and Restaurant Week offered 2-for-1 show deals and prix-fixe menus—perfect for date nights.

NYNightlife Tips for This Week

  • Festive rooftop? Book early at 230 Fifth to catch the skyline mood post‑NYE.
  • Bundle up! Winter Village is magical—but bring gloves and a coat for the rink.
  • Free culture: Gospel Sundays and community parades are great for immersive local experiences.
  • Date idea: Pair a Broadway show with a prix‑fixe dinner deal—budget‑friendly and classy.
